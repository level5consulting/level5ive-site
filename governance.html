G
"Is your AI deployed responsibly?"

The core problem

Most organizations are licensing AI tools faster than they're governing them. No one owns AI governance. No one tracks AI cost at the function level. The spend accumulates — the value doesn't.

The typical result

Shadow AI proliferates. Costs hide under the lampshade. The CFO asks whether the investment is worth it — and no one can answer with data. The reckoning is coming.

What governance actually does

It creates the value discipline that makes AI investments accountable. Not as a brake — as a flight yoke. The right governance framework lets you move faster with confidence.

The 7 Principles

What it means to govern AI well.

Seven principles that together create an AI governance system — not a policy document, not a committee, but an operating discipline that runs continuously.

1

Owned by a Dedicated Function — The Chief AI Evangelist

AI governance without ownership is a policy document. The GO Framework places governance in the hands of a dedicated Chief AI Evangelist — a role that bridges functional knowledge, change velocity, and value accountability. Without this, governance stalls at the committee stage. With it, the whole system moves.

Foundation Principle
2

Right AI for the Right Job — Hire AI Like You Hire People

Not every AI tool belongs in every function. The GO Framework applies the same rigor to AI selection as to talent acquisition — matching capability to need, evaluating fit, and making deliberate deployment decisions rather than licensing everything that ships.

Selection Discipline
3

Federated by Function — One Size Fits None

A centralized AI policy handed down from IT serves no function well. The GO Framework federates governance — each function owns its AI decisions within a shared accountability structure. Marketing's AI needs look nothing like Finance's. Governance should reflect that reality.

Structure Principle
4

Governance as Velocity — Absorb and Disseminate Change at Pace

AI is moving faster than any governance framework built for annual review cycles can handle. The GO Framework builds governance as a real-time capability — absorbing new tools, models, and risks as they emerge and disseminating decisions at pace across the organization.

Velocity Principle
5

True Cost Visibility — Bring Costs Out From Under the Lampshade

AI costs are diffuse by nature — spread across SaaS subscriptions, embedded tools, API usage, and productivity overhead. The GO Framework surfaces the true cost of AI at the function level, so leadership can make investment decisions with real numbers rather than optimistic estimates.

Financial Discipline
6

Function-Specific Value Metrics — Match Cost to Value

Measuring AI value at the enterprise level obscures where it's working and where it isn't. The GO Framework builds function-specific metrics that match the cost of AI to the value it produces in that context — creating accountability without bureaucracy.

Measurement Principle
7

Data-Driven Retirement — Say No as a Natural Output of the Metrics

Most organizations have no mechanism for retiring AI tools that aren't performing. The GO Framework makes retirement a natural output of the value metrics — when a tool consistently fails to justify its cost in a function, the data says no. No politics. No inertia. Just discipline.

Lifecycle Principle

Right AI for the Right Job

Not all AI is the same.
Governance starts with knowing the difference.

Principle 2 in practice: the GO Framework distinguishes between general-purpose LLMs and purpose-built AI tools — and deploys each where it actually belongs.

General-Purpose LLMs
Broad capability · High flexibility · Requires prompt discipline
  • ChatGPT / GPT-4o
    OpenAI · General reasoning, writing, analysis
  • Microsoft Copilot
    Microsoft 365 · Embedded productivity across Office suite
  • Claude
    Anthropic · Long-context reasoning, document analysis
  • Gemini
    Google · Multimodal, integrated with Google Workspace
Purpose-Built AI Tools
Deep domain expertise · Structured outputs · Governed by design
  • Planview Anvi
    Portfolio intelligence · AI-assisted resource and delivery insights
  • Planisware Prisma
    Project AI · Predictive scheduling and risk detection
  • Atlassian Rovo
    Knowledge AI · Connects Jira, Confluence, and team knowledge
  • ServiceNow Otto
    Workflow AI · Automates IT and enterprise service processes

The governance question isn't which AI to use — it's whether you have a principled framework for deciding. Without Principle 2, organizations default to whatever the loudest vendor demo looked like. With it, every AI deployment is a deliberate decision matched to functional need and accountable to value metrics.

Where Organizations Are Headed

The AI reckoning is not a prediction.
It's a pattern.

Every major technology wave has followed the same four stages. AI is no different — the only variable is which stage your organization is in when the reckoning arrives.

📈
Stage 1
Accumulation

Tools proliferate. Budgets flow. Every function licenses something. Enthusiasm is high, governance is low, and no one is tracking the full cost picture.

💡
Stage 2
Illumination

The CFO starts asking questions. Shadow AI surfaces. The gap between AI spend and AI value becomes visible — and uncomfortable.

⚡
Stage 3 · Most orgs are here
Reckoning

Boards demand accountability. Budgets get cut. Organizations without a governance framework scramble to justify what they've spent. Some don't survive it intact.

✓
Stage 4 · The destination
Discipline

Organizations with governance already in place move through the reckoning cleanly. They have the data, the metrics, and the language to defend every AI investment — and double down on what works.
