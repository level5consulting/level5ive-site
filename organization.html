The 6 Principles

Building the human capability
that makes governance work.

Six principles that build organizational readiness — not as a one-time change program, but as a permanent competency that grows with AI.

1

Build the AI Evangelism Function — Chief AI Evangelist + Team

The Chief AI Evangelist is not a title — it's a function. This role sits at the intersection of AI capability, business strategy, and human change. The GO Framework defines exactly what this function looks like, how it's staffed, and how it scales — from a single evangelist in a mid-size organization to a full team in an enterprise.

Foundation Principle
2

The BRM Model for AI — Deep Functional Knowledge Required

AI evangelists can't be generalists. The GO Framework applies the Business Relationship Manager model — each AI evangelist is embedded in a specific function (Finance, Operations, HR, Sales) and develops genuine domain expertise. A Finance AI evangelist who doesn't understand the month-end close will never earn the room's trust.

Capability Principle
3

WIIFM as the Change Tool — The 5 Acts Framework

"What's in it for me?" is not a resistance tactic — it's the only question that drives sustainable adoption. The GO Framework uses the 5 Acts journey as the change methodology, meeting each person where they are and moving them forward individually. Org-level change is the sum of individual-level change.

Change Principle
4

Velocity as a Competency — Absorb Change at Pace

The organizations that win with AI are not the ones that move fastest on day one — they're the ones that can absorb change continuously over the long term. The GO Framework treats velocity as an organizational muscle that's built deliberately, measured quarterly, and grown over time.

Velocity Principle
5

Individual-Level Change — Every Person at a Different Act

A VP of Finance and a junior analyst are not at the same point in their AI journey. Treating them as if they are produces compliance, not capability. The GO Framework tracks and manages change at the individual level — every person gets a personalized path through the 5 Acts, not a one-size change program.

Individual Principle
6

Personal AI Readiness Assessment — 5 Dimensions, 90-Day Rhythm

You can't manage what you can't measure. The GO Framework includes a Personal AI Readiness Assessment across five dimensions — run on a 90-day cycle so progress is tracked, stalls are visible, and the organization always knows where its AI readiness actually stands.

Measurement Principle

Principle 1 in Practice

What the AI Evangelism function actually looks like.

The Chief AI Evangelist owns the bridge between AI capability and business adoption. Below that, function-embedded evangelists translate AI into the language and reality of each business domain.

The model scales — a small organization might start with a single Chief AI Evangelist. An enterprise might have a team of six. The structure is the same; the depth varies.

Principle 3 in Practice

The 5 Acts of AI Adoption.

Every individual moves through these five stages on their AI journey. The GO Framework meets them where they are — not where the organization wants them to be.

1
Act 1
Anxiety

AI feels threatening, unclear, or irrelevant. The individual is waiting to be told what to think.

2
Act 2
Grind

Experimenting but struggling. AI tools feel like more work, not less. Frustration is common.

3
Act 3 · The Shift
Hero Moment

One breakthrough experience reframes everything. AI stops feeling like a threat and starts feeling like leverage.

4
Act 4
Value Hypothesis

Actively building AI into workflows. Starting to see measurable value — and starting to advocate for it.

5
Act 5 · The Goal
Outcome Realized

AI is a natural part of how this person works. They're an informal evangelist — pulling others through the journey.

The GO Framework tracks every individual's Act. When you know where every person is, you can design the right intervention at the right moment — instead of running a change program that hits Act 5 people with Act 1 content and wonders why adoption stalls.

Principle 6 in Practice

Personal AI Readiness Assessment.

Five dimensions. Measured at the individual level. Run on a 90-day cycle so progress is visible and stalls are caught early — not discovered in an annual review.

A

Awareness

Does this person understand what AI is, what it can and can't do, and where it's already in use in their function?

A

Attitude

How does this person feel about AI — threatened, curious, indifferent? Attitude predicts adoption before any training happens.

A

Aptitude

What is this person's current capability to use AI tools effectively in their role? Where are the specific skill gaps?

A

Application

Is this person actually applying AI in their day-to-day work? Capability without application produces no value.

A

Advocacy

Is this person sharing AI wins, coaching peers, and pulling others through the journey? Advocacy is the multiplier.

Why 90 days — not annual.

Annual assessments tell you where people were. They don't help you catch the person stuck at Act 2 before they become a blocker, or recognize the Act 4 advocate before they leave for a competitor who noticed them.

The 90-day rhythm creates a governance loop — assess, intervene, measure, repeat. Each cycle builds on the last. After a year, you have four data points per person and a genuine picture of organizational AI readiness velocity.

The 90-Day Readiness Cycle

Day 1

Individual assessment across 5 dimensions — baseline score established

Days 2–89

Targeted interventions based on dimension gaps — evangelist-led, function-specific

Day 90

Reassessment — delta measured, new cycle designed, progress reported to Chief AI Evangelist
